Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term changes in temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric conditions on Earth. While natural factors contribute to climate variability, the term "climate change" is often used to describe the anthropogenic (human-induced) changes that have been occurring since the Industrial Revolution.
    Greenhouse Gas Emissions: The burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas), deforestation, industrial processes, and certain agricultural practices release greenhouse gases (GHGs) into the atmosphere. The primary greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), and fluorinated gases. These gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to the greenhouse effect.
Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels: Warming temperatures contribute to the melting of glaciers, ice caps, and polar ice sheets. This results in rising sea levels, posing risks to coastal regions and low-lying areas. Melting Arctic sea ice has also been observed, affecting ecosystems and wildlife in the region.
